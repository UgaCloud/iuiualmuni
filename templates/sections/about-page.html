About IUIU and Its Alumni Association

The Islamic University in Uganda (IUIU) was established on 10 February 1988 through a collaboration between the Organisation of Islamic Cooperation (OIC) and the Government of Uganda. It was formalized by the Parliament of Uganda in 1990 and has since grown into a leading higher education institution serving students across East Africa and beyond.

The IUIU Alumni Association (IUIU‑AA) was formally registered on 12 August 2020 with the Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB). Its purpose is to unite former students under one umbrella, create fellowship, and support both alumni and their Alma Mater through networking, career development, mentorship, and collaborative initiatives.

2020

Our Vision and Mission

Vision: Unity and development among alumni and the broader IUIU community.

Mission: To create fellowship among IUIU Alumni under one umbrella and offer practical solutions that benefit members and the Alma Mater through rigorous networking, mentorship, and engagement.

Core Values: Integrity, Diversity, Creativity, and Coexistence guide all activities and outreach.

2023

Alumni Engagement & Community Impact

The IUIU Alumni Association has launched several impactful initiatives, including scholarship programs for academically talented but needy students and a major campaign to empower teachers from Muslim-founded schools to obtain university degrees by 2030.

Through annual alumni dinners, expos, networking events, and mentorship programs, IUIU‑AA strengthens alumni connections while supporting educational advancement and community development in Uganda and beyond.
